# RecipeScanner — Demo Video Script

**Estimated duration:** 2:30 minutes

---

## [0:00 – 0:10] — Intro

- Black screen with text: *"RecipeScanner — AI-Powered Recipe Management"*
- Subtext: *"Portfolio Project · Holberton School 2026"*
- Black background, white text, orange accent

---

## [0:10 – 0:25] — Home screen

- Open the app in the browser, already logged in
- Show the home screen with total estimated cost, most expensive recipes, and top ingredients ranking
- Caption: *"Your recipe collection — cost-estimated at a glance"*

---

## [0:25 – 1:00] — Scan a PDF

- Navigate to "Scan Recipe"
- Drag or select the PDF file (have a recipe ready)
- Click "Scan with AI"
- Show the spinner while processing
- Success message appears with the recipe name
- Caption: *"Upload any recipe PDF → structured data in seconds"*

---

## [1:00 – 1:30] — View the scanned recipe

- Click "View recipe"
- Show the recipe page:
  - Title, category, prep time, servings
  - Ingredient list with quantities and units
  - Numbered preparation steps
- Caption: *"Ingredients, steps, and metadata — extracted automatically"*

---

## [1:30 – 1:50] — Language switch

- With the recipe open, click **ES** → all content switches to Spanish
- Then **FR** → switches to French
- Then back to **EN**
- Caption: *"One click — English, Spanish, or French"*

---

## [1:50 – 2:15] — Recipe cost

- On the same recipe page, scroll to the ingredients section
- Show the price per ingredient and the total estimated cost
- Caption: *"Real cost estimation — per ingredient and total"*

---

## [2:15 – 2:30] — Closing

- Go back to home, show the updated summary with the new recipe
- Final screen with text:
  - *"RecipeScanner"*
  - *"github.com/juliangf94/recipe-scanner"*
  - *"recipes-scanner.netlify.app"*

---

## Tips before recording

- Clear browser history so no strange URLs appear
- Use a PDF recipe you know scans correctly
- Set browser to full screen (F11)
- Record without audio first — add background music in editor if needed
- In OBS: Settings → Output → Recording Format → MP4
